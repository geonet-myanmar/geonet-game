## Study Strategies

### 1. Spaced Repetition

**Method:**
- Play Beginner level first
- Wait 1 day, play Intermediate
- Wait 2-3 days, play Advanced
- Return to difficult categories

**Why it works**: Spacing sessions improves long-term retention.

---

### 2. Active Recall

**Method:**
- Read question
- Try to answer BEFORE looking at options
- Select your answer
- Review feedback carefully

**Why it works**: Retrieving information strengthens memory more than passive reading.

---

### 3. Interleaving

**Method:**
- Rotate between categories in single session
- Don't master one category before moving to next
- Mix difficulties

**Why it works**: Prevents fatigue and improves discrimination between concepts.

---

### 4. Focus on Mistakes

**Method:**
- Note which questions you miss
- Review explanations carefully
- Revisit those topics in textbooks
- Replay category to test improvement

**Why it works**: Learning from errors is more effective than reviewing known material.

---

### 5. Pre-Exam Preparation

**Week Before Exam:**
- Play all relevant categories on Beginner
- Focus on weak areas

**3 Days Before:**
- Intermediate difficulty for all topics
- Note persistent weak areas

**Day Before:**
- Quick rounds on Beginner for confidence
- Advanced on strongest topics

**Day Of:**
- Light review, 1-2 quick rounds
- Build confidence, don't cram

## Tips & Tricks

### Maximize Your Score

1. **Build Streaks Early**
   - Answer first few questions correctly to establish multiplier
   - Even +2-3 streak adds significant points

2. **Speed Matters on Easy Questions**
   - Answer obvious questions quickly for speed bonus
   - Take full time on challenging questions

3. **Learn from Feedback**
   - Read explanations even when correct
   - Deepen understanding for future questions

4. **Track Your Progress**
   - Watch progress bars fill
   - Replay categories at 50-70% to boost completion

5. **Use Keyboard Shortcuts**
   - Much faster than clicking
   - 1-4 or A-D for MC, T/F for true/false

---

### Effective Learning

1. **Don't Memorize Questions**
   - Focus on understanding concepts
   - Questions randomize; principles remain constant

2. **Use as Pre-Lecture Primer**
   - Play before class on relevant topic
   - Activates prior knowledge
   - Makes lecture more meaningful

3. **Review After Lectures**
   - Reinforce new material within 24 hours
   - Solidifies understanding

4. **Study Groups**
   - Share screen and discuss answers
   - Explain reasoning to peers
   - Learn from others' thought processes

5. **Supplement, Don't Replace**
   - GeoNet is a study tool, not a textbook replacement
   - Use alongside readings, lectures, and labs

### Visual Glitches

**Symptoms:** Overlapping text, misaligned elements, broken layout

**Solutions:**

2. Increase browser window size
3. Refresh page (F5 or Cmd+R)
4. Clear browser cache
5. Update browser to latest version

---

### Questions Repeating

**Symptoms:** Same questions appear multiple times in one round

**Note:** This is unlikely but possible due to random selection from pool.

**Solutions:**
- Questions randomly selected from pool
- Pools have 10-12 questions each
- Game draws 10 questions per round
- Some overlap possible on replays
- Not a bugâ€”ensures variety across sessions

### Lost High Score

**Symptoms:** High score disappeared

**Causes:**
- Browser cache cleared
- LocalStorage cleared
- Clicked "Reset Progress"
- Switched browsers (data is browser-specific)

**Prevention:**
- Don't clear browser data if you want to keep scores
- Use same browser consistently
- Be careful with "Reset Progress" button
