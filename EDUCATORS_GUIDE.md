## Overview for Educators

### What is GeoNet?

GeoNet is a free, open-source web-based quiz game designed to help undergraduate geology students master key concepts through active recall and spaced repetition.

### Why Use GeoNet?

**Evidence-Based Learning:**
- âœ… Active recall strengthens memory formation
- âœ… Immediate feedback accelerates learning
- âœ… Gamification increases engagement
- âœ… Self-paced allows differentiated instruction
- âœ… Spaced repetition improves long-term retention

**Practical Benefits:**
- âœ… No setup required (single HTML file)
- âœ… Works on any device (desktop, tablet, mobile)
- âœ… No accounts or registration needed
- âœ… Free and open source
- âœ… Completely private (no data collection)
- âœ… Works offline

### Learning Objectives Supported

Students will:
1. Recall and identify key geological terms and concepts
2. Apply geological principles to solve problems
3. Analyze relationships between geological processes
4. Synthesize knowledge across multiple topics
5. Evaluate geological scenarios using learned criteria

## Classroom Integration

### In-Class Activities

#### 1. **Pre-Lecture Primer** (5-10 minutes)
**Purpose**: Activate prior knowledge before introducing new material

**Implementation:**
- Students play 1 round on topic to be covered
- Don't require high scoresâ€”goal is activation
- Discuss common misconceptions revealed
- Use results to gauge starting knowledge

**Example:**
> "Before we discuss plate tectonics, spend 5 minutes playing the Plate Tectonics category on beginner difficulty. We'll discuss common challenges you encountered."

---

#### 2. **Post-Lecture Review** (10-15 minutes)
**Purpose**: Reinforce concepts just taught

**Implementation:**
- Immediately after lecture, students play relevant category
- Appropriate difficulty based on lecture depth
- Students discuss missed questions in pairs
- Instructor addresses common errors

**Example:**
> "We just covered metamorphic processes. Now play the Processes category on intermediate difficulty, focusing on metamorphism questions. Raise your hand if you encounter a question you can't answer."

---

#### 3. **Competitive Review Game** (20-30 minutes)
**Purpose**: Energize review sessions, build friendly competition

**Implementation:**
- Project GeoNet on screen
- Teams take turns answering
- Award points for speed and accuracy
- Discuss each answer before moving on

**Setup:**
- Divide class into 4-5 teams
- Each team selects a representative per question
- Use timer for added pressure
- Keep score on whiteboard

**Example:**
> "Let's have a GeoNet tournament! Team A, send someone to the front. You have 15 seconds to answer this mineral identification question."

---

#### 4. **Error Analysis Workshop** (15-20 minutes)
**Purpose**: Deep learning through mistake analysis

**Implementation:**
- Students play individually, noting missed questions
- Small groups discuss why wrong answers were appealing
- Groups identify knowledge gaps
- Instructor provides targeted mini-lessons

**Example:**
> "Play the Geologic Time category. For each question you miss, write down: (1) What you answered, (2) Why you chose it, (3) Why the correct answer is right. We'll discuss common misconceptions."

---

#### 5. **Jigsaw Learning** (30-40 minutes)
**Purpose**: Peer teaching for deep understanding

**Implementation:**
- Assign each student a different category
- Students become "experts" via GeoNet practice
- Regroup to teach others
- Everyone plays mixed quiz to assess knowledge transfer

**Steps:**
1. Form expert groups by category (5 min)
2. Experts master their topic via GeoNet (10 min)
3. Regroup into teaching teams (1 expert per category) (5 min)
4. Experts teach their topic (15 min)
5. Mixed quiz assessment (5 min)

### Homework Assignments

#### Assignment 1: **Spaced Practice Journal**
**Duration**: 2 weeks

**Instructions:**
- Play 1 category per day (10 questions each)
- Maintain journal with:
  - Date and time
  - Category and difficulty
  - Score and accuracy
  - 2-3 challenging concepts encountered
  - Self-reflection on improvement

**Grading**: Completion-based, reward consistency

---

**Instructions:**

- Play beginner â†’ intermediate â†’ advanced

- Must achieve 60% on intermediate before moving to advanced

**Grading**: Based on progression and final advanced score

**Grading**: Accuracy, clarity, educational value

**Benefits**: Forces deep engagement with material, develops assessment literacy

## Pedagogical Approach

### Active Learning Principles

**1. Retrieval Practice**
- GeoNet forces students to recall information
- More effective than passive re-reading
- Strengthens memory pathways

**2. Interleaving**
- Mixed question types prevent autopilot
- Improves discrimination between concepts
- Mirrors real-world geological problem-solving

**3. Immediate Feedback**
- Students learn from mistakes in real-time
- Explanations provide corrective instruction
- Prevents consolidation of misconceptions

**4. Spaced Repetition**
- Encourage regular play over cramming
- Long-term retention improves with spacing
- Progress tracking shows incremental improvement

**5. Formative Assessment**
- Low-stakes practice reduces anxiety
- Reveals knowledge gaps for targeted study
- Students self-regulate learning pace

## Lesson Plans

### Sample Lesson 1: Introduction to Minerals

**Course**: Introductory Geology
**Duration**: 50 minutes
**Topic**: Mineral identification and properties

#### Learning Objectives
- Define minerals and their properties
- Identify diagnostic properties (hardness, luster, cleavage)
- Apply Mohs scale to mineral identification

#### Materials
- Mineral specimens
- GeoNet (computers/tablets)
- Mohs hardness scale chart

#### Procedure

**Engage (5 min)**
- Show mystery mineral
- Ask: "How would you identify this?"
- Introduce challenge: Learn to ID minerals scientifically

**Explore (10 min)**
- Students play Mineral & Rock ID category (Beginner)
- Note: Play without instruction first
- Observe what properties appear in questions

**Explain (20 min)**
- Lecture on mineral properties
  - Hardness (Mohs scale)
  - Luster (metallic, vitreous, pearly)
  - Cleavage vs. fracture
  - Color and streak
  - Crystal form
- Reference questions students encountered

**Elaborate (10 min)**
- Students replay Mineral & Rock ID (Beginner)
- Should see improved scores
- Discuss how knowledge application improved performance

**Evaluate (5 min)**
- Exit ticket: "Name 3 diagnostic mineral properties and explain how each helps with identification"
- Assign: Play Mineral & Rock ID (Beginner) until 80%+ accuracy

---

### Sample Lesson 2: Plate Tectonics Review

**Course**: Physical Geology
**Duration**: 75 minutes (lab period)
**Topic**: Comprehensive plate tectonics review before exam

#### Learning Objectives
- Synthesize plate tectonic concepts
- Apply knowledge to real-world examples
- Identify personal knowledge gaps

#### Materials
- GeoNet (computers)
- World map
- Note-taking supplies

#### Procedure

**Diagnostic (15 min)**
- Students play Plate Tectonics (Intermediate)
- Record missed questions
- Identify 3 weakest topics

**Targeted Mini-Lessons (30 min)**
- Group students by weak topics
- Station rotation:
  - Station 1: Boundary types (instructor-led)
  - Station 2: Seafloor spreading (peer-led)
  - Station 3: Mountain building (video + discussion)
  - Station 4: Real-world examples (map activity)
- 7-8 min per station

**Reassessment (15 min)**
- Students replay Plate Tectonics (Intermediate)
- Compare scores to diagnostic
- Celebrate improvements

**Application (10 min)**
- Advanced students: Play Plate Tectonics (Advanced)
- Others: Review explanations for missed questions
- Pairs discuss most challenging concepts

**Closure (5 min)**
- Whole-class discussion: Most interesting/surprising fact learned
- Preview exam format
- Assign: Daily GeoNet practice until exam

## Assessment Strategies

### Formative Assessment

**Use GeoNet for:**
- âœ… Pre-assessments (gauge prior knowledge)
- âœ… Progress monitoring (track improvement)
- âœ… Self-assessment (students identify weak areas)
- âœ… Peer discussion prompts
- âœ… Homework completion checks

**Do NOT use GeoNet for:**
- âŒ High-stakes grades (too easy to "game")
- âŒ Direct points toward course grade (reduces intrinsic motivation)
- âŒ Punitive measures

---

### Suggested Grading Approach

**Option 1: Completion-Based (Recommended)**
- Award points for completing assigned rounds
- Focus on effort and consistency, not scores
- Example: "Play 5 rounds this week on any category = 10/10 homework points"

**Option 2: Improvement-Based**
- Grade based on score improvement over time
- Rewards growth mindset
- Example: "Improve your average score by 20% = full credit"

**Option 3: Reflection-Based**
- Students submit reflection on what they learned
- Graded on thoughtfulness, not scores
- Example: "Write 1-page reflection on GeoNet practice and concepts you struggled with"

**Option 4: Low-Stakes Quizzes**
- Use GeoNet to prepare for in-class quizzes
- Quiz questions similar to (but not identical to) GeoNet
- GeoNet practice optional but highly beneficial

---

### Extra Credit Opportunities

- **Score Threshold**: Achieve 90%+ on Advanced difficulty = +2% on next exam
- **Question Contribution**: Submit 3 quality questions = +1% on final grade
- **Peer Teaching**: Tutor classmate using GeoNet, both document improvement = +1%
- **Challenge Mode**: Complete all categories at all difficulties in one week = +3%

## Technical Setup

### For Computer Labs

**Steps:**
1. Download `index.html` to network drive or local storage
2. Create desktop shortcut for easy access
3. Ensure JavaScript enabled on all computers
4. Test on one machine before class

**Advantages:**
- No internet required (works offline)
- No software installation needed
- Works on old computers (low system requirements)
- No user accounts to manage

---

### For BYOD (Bring Your Own Device)

**Option 1: Direct Download**
- Share file via course LMS (Blackboard, Canvas, Moodle)
- Students download and open locally
- Works offline for studying anywhere

**Option 2: Web Hosting**
- Host on GitHub Pages (free)
- Students access via URL
- Updates automatically push to all students

**Option 3: USB Distribution**
- Copy to USB drives
- Students transfer to personal devices
- Good for low-connectivity environments

---

### For Projector/Interactive Whiteboard

**Steps:**
1. Open GeoNet on instructor computer
2. Project on screen
3. Use for whole-class activities
4. Consider wireless mouse for student interaction

**Tips:**
- Increase browser zoom (Ctrl +) for visibility
- Use fullscreen mode (F11)
- Enable "Presenter Mode" if available

---

### Dealing with Resistance

**Common Student Objections:**

**"I don't like games / I prefer traditional studying"**
- Response: GeoNet is evidence-based active recall, proven more effective than passive reading
- Offer alternative: Use GeoNet as quiz practice, not "game"

**"I don't have time"**
- Response: 10 questions take 3-5 minutes; short sessions are effective
- Suggest: Use between classes, during commute, before bed

**"The questions are too hard/easy"**
- Response: Choose appropriate difficulty; start beginner and progress
- Solution: Mix difficulties for optimal challenge

**"My score doesn't improve"**
- Response: Focus on explanations, not just answers; review textbook for missed topics
- Encourage: Improvement takes time; consistency matters more than immediate results

## Troubleshooting

### Technical Issues

**"GeoNet won't load"**
- Check JavaScript is enabled
- Try different browser
- Disable browser extensions
- Ensure file isn't corrupted (re-download)

**"Progress isn't saving"**
- Check localStorage isn't disabled
- Not in Incognito/Private mode
- Browser isn't automatically clearing data
- Try different browser

**"Drag-and-drop doesn't work"**
- Update browser to latest version
- Try different browser (Safari sometimes has issues)
- Use desktop version (better drag support)

---

### Pedagogical Issues

**"Students are just memorizing answers"**
- Rotate question pools (add new questions)
- Emphasize understanding explanations
- Pair GeoNet with application tasks (labs, fieldwork)
- Use GeoNet for prep, not direct assessment

**"High-performing students find it too easy"**
- Direct them to Advanced difficulty
- Assign question creation project
- Use as pre-assessment, excuse from additional homework
- Encourage peer tutoring using GeoNet

**"Low-performing students are discouraged"**
- Start with Beginner difficulty
- Emphasize growth, not absolute scores
- Provide additional resources (tutoring, office hours)
- Pair with peer study groups
- Celebrate small improvements

**"Students only play right before exams"**
- Assign regular weekly practice (small points)
- Discuss benefits of spaced repetition
- Incorporate in-class so students experience benefits
- Make non-punitive (completion-based grading)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use GeoNet in a fully online course?**
A: Yes! Share file via LMS or host on website. Students can practice independently.

**Q: How do I prevent cheating?**
A: Don't use GeoNet for high-stakes assessment. Use for formative practice and pair with proctored exams.

**Q: Can I modify questions to match my textbook?**
A: Yes! See [DEVELOPER_GUIDE.md](DEVELOPER_GUIDE.md) for instructions. Please consider contributing your questions back.

**Q: Do students need accounts?**
A: No accounts required. All data stored locally in browser.

**Q: Can I track individual student performance?**
A: Not automatically. Use self-reporting methods described in [Student Management](#student-management).

**Q: Is there a gradebook integration?**
A: Not currently. GeoNet is designed for formative assessment. Consider requesting this feature if needed.

**Q: Can I use this for K-12 education?**
A: Content is targeted at undergraduates, but advanced high school students may benefit, especially from beginner difficulty.

**Q: How often should students use GeoNet?**
A: Research suggests daily short sessions (5-10 min) are more effective than weekly long sessions.

**Q: What if my institution blocks JavaScript?**
A: GeoNet requires JavaScript. Contact IT to whitelist or use personal devices.
